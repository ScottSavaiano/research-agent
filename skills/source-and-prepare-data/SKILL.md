## What this skill is

The research agent's **execution-regime real-data stage**: it takes the **already-validated, already-named dataset** — selected and construct-validated in planning by `operationalize-construct` (Stage 6.5) and named in the Stage-12 methodology — and converts it into a **real, analysis-ready dataset plus a written record of where it came from and how it was prepared.** The mentor's planning-time reachability check asks *"can we get data for this?"*; `operationalize-construct` answers *"does this dataset actually measure it, and how?"*; this skill *gets it, prepares it, and QCs it.*

It is the execution counterpart of `operationalize-construct`, and the **real-data** sibling of `generate-synthetic-data`:

- `operationalize-construct` (planning) — decides *how each construct is measured and in which dataset, and proves the measurement is sound*; may explore on a **toy/synthetic** surface that is **never analyzed and never validates** anything.
- **`source-and-prepare-data` (execution, this skill)** — **acquires the real dataset**, cleans it, QCs it. It handles **public research datasets only** and **never invents data**.
- `generate-synthetic-data` (execution) — generates synthetic/simulated data **as the legitimate studied object** when the sealed methodology calls for it (shape E, and the cross-shape synthetic uses), validated by algorithmic fidelity. That is a different skill; this one routes there and does not do it.

By completion the student has:

- A **cleaned dataset** staged in the workspace `data/` folder, with the exact subset/variables the analysis needs.
- A **data-provenance & preparation record** (`data/data-provenance.md`) recording: source + access path, every cleaning decision, the analytic N, and the QC checks — the trail the Methods/Data section and the review agent both draw on. It **carries forward** the construct→variable mapping and the construct-validity verdict produced upstream by `operationalize-construct`, rather than re-deriving them.
- Anything the *acquired data* reveals that should **loop back** — e.g., a variable that, on the real file, does not behave as the codebook promised. Post-seal, that is **refined against the data and reported as a deviation** in the as-executed Methods (§6), never a silent rewrite of the frozen hypotheses.

It never invents data, never fabricates values for missing cases, and (per program privacy rules) handles **public research datasets only** — student records are never training or analysis fodder for shared work.

## When this skill fires

In the research agent's **execution regime**, after the proposal is **sealed** (Stage 14 — the methodology, including the named-and-validated dataset, is frozen) and before the analysis runs (Stages 16–18, as-executed). It is the **first execution-regime skill**: the cycle seal hands the student out of the mentor-led planning regime into execution, which is the research agent's continuous lane.

It can re-fire if acquisition reveals a measurement problem (below), and it cedes to `generate-synthetic-data` when the sealed method is a synthetic/simulation design.

**Why acquisition and cleaning wait for the seal (deliberate, not an oversight).** Planning (Stage 6.5, `operationalize-construct`) deliberately validates the operationalization on a **codebook** (and at most a small sample or a labeled toy set), not on a cleaned analytic file — because acquiring and cleaning the full dataset before the seal would be **wasted effort** if the design still changes (a failed validity check bounces back to revise the research question; the §7 stale map cascades). The seal is the commitment point that makes the one-time heavy data work worth doing. So the division is **validation (planning, cheap, revisable)** versus **preparation (execution, post-seal, committed)** — not an arbitrary stage boundary.

## Dispatch and the handoff (the execution-regime shape)

This is the load-bearing difference from `operationalize-construct`, which lives in planning and returns the student to the mentor. This skill lives in **execution**, the research agent's own regime.

- **Inbound — seal-dispatched.** At the cycle seal (Stage 14) the **mentor composes the concrete handoff into execution** (the R1/R10 pattern: the concrete switch to the research agent — icon-click on desktop; `research` on the TUI — plus a prompt naming the sealed methodology and the validated dataset from the Stage-6.5 record). The research agent recognizes the arriving handoff per R1/R9 and reads the named files first.
- **Forward, not back.** On a clean completion this skill does **not** return the student to the mentor (planning's pattern). Execution stays the research agent's lane, so it hands **forward to analysis** (`choose-analysis-method` / `run-analysis`, Tier 6), which preconditions on this skill's prepared dataset + record.
- **R11 review-agent spot-checks along the way (two named points).** After a consequential execution task the research agent initiates a concrete handoff sending the student to the **review agent** for AI-error spot-checks (R11; V5 function 2) — the *sanity-check stopping points*. This skill has two:
  - **after Phase 1 (download integrity)** — "are these the right rows/waves? did the download skip records? does the file match the codebook's N?";
  - **after Phase 2 (cleaning)** — "how were missing values handled? did the recodes do what the log says? did the CSV load the corpus into the right cells?"
  These are exactly the checks V5 function 2 names; routing them to the dedicated guard (rather than self-checking) is the point.
- **The measurement-failure loop-back.** If preparation reveals the data does not carry a construct as validated, the handoff loops **back** — to `operationalize-construct` (if a planning revision is still open) or, **post-seal**, refined against the data and reported as a §6 deviation in the as-executed Methods, never a silent rewrite of the frozen hypotheses.

## What "prepare" means depends on the method — the menu-shape layer

The three-phase spine below is **menu-agnostic** — access the real data, prepare it, QC it — but what "prepare" concretely produces depends on the method the student chose at Stage 1. The shapes mirror `operationalize-construct`'s six (same letters, so the two skills line up across planning and execution):

**A. Map construct → variable/index in existing data** — *secondary survey/administrative datasets.* Prepare: download the dataset; recode variables, handle missing data, build derived indices, restrict to the analytic sample. QC: distributions/ranges + **reproduce a published benchmark** (a known prevalence or mean). *(Fully specified below — gold exemplar #1; YRBS/MTF lineage.)*

**B. Operationalize the identification** — *quasi-experimental designs on observational data.* Prepare: assemble the identification machinery's **data inputs** — the running variable + cutoff (RD), the treatment-timing panel (DiD), the instrument, the donor pool (synthetic control) — and shape the file so the assumption checks are computable (a clean pre-period, density around the cutoff). The assumption *tests* are analysis; the *data shaped to support them* is here. QC: panel balance, pre-period coverage, cutoff-density sanity. *(Sketch; per-exemplar.)*

**C. Build a constructed quantity** — *index-building in econometrics / social epi / geo.* Prepare: access the source data, then **construct the object by the stated formula** (rank-rank slope, shift-share exposure, redlining overlay) — the derived-variable step is the heart. QC: reproduce a published value of the constructed quantity; panel balance. *(Sketch; per-exemplar.)*

**D. Measurement-model corpus** — *NLP/computational text; ML-as-measure; networks.* Prepare: **assemble the corpus** (scrape/API under the R7 reachability rules + the consent-to-publicness IRB line), then the **annotation/labeling pipeline + a gold-label set** (text), the feature/target table (ML), or the tie-list + structural measure (networks). QC: inter-annotator reliability / held-out accuracy of the extraction; network homophily-vs-influence sanity. *(Sketch; per-exemplar.)*

**E. Synthetic / simulation — NOT this skill.** *Synthetic AI survey participants, generative-agent simulation.* Here "sourcing data" *is generating it*, and the synthetic data is the **legitimate analyzed object** — a different integrity regime (validated by algorithmic fidelity, not "never analyzed"). Routed to **`generate-synthetic-data`** (execution). This skill never generates analyzed data. *(See the cross-skill note below.)*

**F. Structure-discovery dataset** — *causal-graphical discovery.* Prepare: assemble the **variable set + the cleaned matrix** the discovery algorithm needs (completeness/missingness suitable for the independence tests). QC: missingness pattern, sample adequacy for the chosen tests. *(Sketch; per-exemplar.)*

**Build-as-you-go flag (load-bearing, mirrors `operationalize-construct`).** Only **shape A is fully specified** here (gold exemplar #1 is observational). **Shapes B, C, D, F are sketches**, each fleshed out to full detail **when a gold exemplar in that shape is built**; **shape E is owned by `generate-synthetic-data`**. Until a shape has an exemplar, a project in it runs the spine using the shape's sketch **plus a declared deferred-scope note** in the data-provenance record — so the gap is explicit, never latent.

> **Cross-skill note — the three-way synthetic-data distinction (so the regimes don't blur):**
> 1. `operationalize-construct` (planning) may build a **toy/synthetic exploration surface** — for *trying* operationalizations only; **never analyzed, never validates** a measure, never enters the paper.
> 2. **This skill** acquires **real data only** — it never generates analyzed data, and if no real dataset is reachable it routes to the research teacher or faculty mentor (gated access), never substitutes synthetic.
> 3. `generate-synthetic-data` (execution) generates synthetic/simulated data **as the legitimate studied object** — only when the **sealed methodology** specifies it (shape E, or a declared cross-shape role: privacy-preserving synthesis, ML augmentation, Monte Carlo/bootstrap for inference stability, simulation for power/robustness) — validated by **algorithmic fidelity** against a real benchmark, and labeled synthetic wherever written.

## The phases (execution — access, prepare, QC)

### Phase 1 — Access, download, and provenance
Obtain the data and record where it came from. Many social-science datasets are **credentialed**: ICPSR (MTF, NSDUH), openICPSR/AEA replication packages, CDC YRBS (public download), UK Data Service (MCS). The skill guides the student through the actual access path — account/registration, the data-use terms they must accept, the specific file/wave to pull — and **records provenance** in `data/data-provenance.md` using the `regeneron-sts-reference.md` §4 fields (source, access date, version/wave, license/terms, citation). It never bypasses a login or terms agreement, and never substitutes a scraped or unofficial copy for the canonical source.

→ **R11 spot-check (download integrity).** Hand the student to the review agent: right rows/waves, no skipped records, file N matches the codebook.

> **Scripted moment — data is gated beyond reach** *(voice-read approved 2026-07-01).* Intent: route to a human, never improvise around an access wall. *Agent:* "This dataset exists, but automatic access is blocked. Here are the specific details of what I need you to work with your research teacher or outside mentor to access directly: _____ While you're doing that, we can make a plan to clean this dataset using the information in its codebook, so we're ready the moment the real file is available."

### Phase 2 — Cleaning
Transform the raw file into the analytic dataset, **recording each decision** (not just doing it): variable recodes and how categories were formed; missing-data handling (and why — listwise, imputation, etc.); derived variables (indices, standardizations); inclusion/exclusion criteria and the resulting **analytic N** (with any subsample Ns). The cleaning log in `data/data-provenance.md` is the spine of the eventual Methods "sample and measures" subsection, and the major cleaning choices also go to `decisions.md` (five-field).

→ **R11 spot-check (cleaning).** Hand the student to the review agent: missing-data handling, recode correctness, the load did what the log claims.

### Phase 3 — Quality control
Before the data is declared analysis-ready: check variable distributions and ranges for impossible values; confirm the analytic N and subsample sizes match expectations; and **benchmark against published numbers** — reproduce a known descriptive (a prevalence, a mean) from the dataset's documentation or a paper that used it, to confirm the extract is correct. Record the QC checks and their outcomes. A failed benchmark sends the agent back to Phase 2 (or Phase 1 if the wrong file was pulled). If the failure is a *measurement* problem — the variable does not capture the construct as validated — it is the loop-back call below.

Because a QC failure can mean either "wrong extract" (fix it) or "the construct isn't really in this data" (a design-level problem), the verdict is a **high-judgment moment** — so at the call the skill surfaces the research agent's **careful-tier** option (the SOUL's "How I run, and when to use a stronger model": suggest switching to the careful tier for this one decision, then dropping back). It does **not** reference the mentor's `model-escalation` meta-skill — that ships only in the project-mentor profile; the research agent's escalation surface is its own SOUL, which keeps the tiers model-agnostic. Fire it once, at the verdict — not per check.

> **Scripted moment — the QC benchmark fails** *(voice-read approved 2026-07-01).* Intent: separate "wrong extract" from "construct not in the data," and slow down for the load-bearing version. *Agent:* "Our benchmark didn't reproduce — the number we should be able to hit off this file isn't coming out. That's good news in a way: QC caught something before it reached your results. Two possibilities, and they're very different. One: we pulled the wrong wave or mis-coded a step — a fix, and we go back a phase. Two: the variable just isn't carrying the construct the way the codebook promised — that's not a cleaning bug, that's a measurement problem, and once we have committed to a specific data analysis approach, we can't rewrite it, so instead let's refine things now against the real data and you can include an explanation of this in your Methods section. Telling those two apart is exactly the kind of call worth slowing down for, so it's worth switching to the careful tier for this one decision. Walk the failed benchmark through with me — what number did we expect, and what did we get?"

## What this skill writes to the workspace

- **`data/data-provenance.md`** — the access/provenance fields, the cleaning log, and the QC results, carrying forward the upstream construct→variable map and construct-validity verdict from `operationalize-construct`. This is the durable execution-track record.
- **`decisions.md`** — the five-field data-preparation decisions (major cleaning/inclusion choices).
- **does NOT write `project_paper_status.md`** (R8 candidate c, 2026-07-01). Execution progress lives in the records above; the paper/position stage advances **mentor-side** at the R10 return (`project-walkthrough` advances the stage; `paper-walkthrough` dispatches `scaffold-section`). The research agent signals via the student's return, never by writing the position file.
- **feeds, does not write — the Methods/Data prose.** The validated operationalization already lives in `project_design.md` (the Stage-6.5 section); the *as-executed* data record is here; the **Methods/Data section is the student's**, written via an **R10 `scaffold-section` handoff** — a **return to the mentor** (the scaffold machinery is single-homed in the project-mentor; this research skill does **not** carry `scaffold-section`) — that draws on this record (function-not-content: the skill writes the log, the student writes the section).

## Cross-agent handoff
Dispatched by the mentor at the seal (R1/R10 inbound). On clean completion, hands **forward** to analysis (within execution), with **R11** review-agent spot-checks at the two stopping points above and an **R10** writing handoff for the Methods/Data section. On a **measurement failure**, the handoff loops **back** to `operationalize-construct` (planning) or is refined-and-reported post-seal (§6); on a **gated-but-real** dataset, it routes the student to the **research teacher or faculty mentor** (institutional access may exist), never forward and never substituted with an unofficial copy. It cedes to **`generate-synthetic-data`** when the sealed method is a synthetic/simulation design (shape E); and where a real-data design **additionally** calls for a declared cross-shape synthetic component (privacy-preserving synthesis, ML augmentation, Monte-Carlo/bootstrap, power/robustness simulation), that component routes to `generate-synthetic-data` while this skill retains the real-data object.

## Edge cases
- **The acquired data does not measure the construct as validated.** The Phase-3 loop-back: pre-seal → `operationalize-construct`; post-seal → refine against the data and report the deviation (§6). Not a silent rewrite of the hypotheses.
- **Data exists but is gated beyond the student's access.** Record the block specifically; route to the research teacher or faculty mentor (institutional access may exist); never substitute an unofficial copy. (Phase-1 scripted moment.)
- **Cleaning would change the construct.** If the only way to make a variable usable distorts what it measures, that is an operationalization/validity problem surfacing late — loop back, don't paper over it.
- **The sealed method needs generated data, not sourced data.** Out of scope by design — cede to `generate-synthetic-data` (shape E and the cross-shape synthetic uses). This skill acquires real data only.
- **Student data / PII.** Out of scope by rule — this skill prepares public research datasets; student-generated data never enters shared analysis or any model. (Scraped/API corpora for shape D follow R7's consent-to-publicness line: identifiable-individual data needs IRB except PII individuals consented to making public.)
