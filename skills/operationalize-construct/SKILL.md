## What this skill is

The research agent's **planning-regime data skill**: it converts the constructs named in the student's three research questions (topic, core-methods, extension-methods) into **concrete, defensible measures — and confirms those measures actually capture the constructs — before the methodology is detailed (Stage 14) and the proposal is sealed (Stage 16).** It is the planning-time counterpart of `source-and-prepare-data`: this skill decides *how each construct will be measured (and, for a secondary dataset, in which dataset) and proves the measurement is sound*; `source-and-prepare-data` later *acquires that data, cleans it, and QCs it* in execution.

The split exists for two reasons, one in each direction. **You cannot seal a proposal around an operationalization you have not validated** — so the validity check must happen in planning. **And you do not acquire and clean the full dataset *before* the seal** — because cleaning is wasted effort if planning still changes the research question or the dataset (the bounce-back; the §7 stale map). So this skill validates on the **codebook** (and at most a small real sample or a labeled toy set), never on a cleaned analytic file; the real dataset is acquired and cleaned only *after* the seal commits the design, by `source-and-prepare-data`. In gold exemplar #1 the dataset decision (switching from Monitoring the Future to YRBS after reading the codebook) is a full five-field decision made in planning — before the methodology named YRBS and before the hypotheses were frozen on the binary "sad or hopeless" outcome. That is this skill's work.

By completion the student has, in the **Data Validity Log** (`data/data-validity-log.md`, cycle stage 5):

- an **operationalization spec** for each construct — naming the exact measure, its units/scale, reference period, and the population/sample it applies to;
- a **measurement-validity verdict** (does the planned measurement capture each construct?);
- the **selected dataset or measurement plan**, with the limitations it carries (e.g., a covariate the dataset lacks);
- anything the check forces **back upstream** — an operationalization that cannot be validly measured, returned to the mentor as a **bounce to Stage 1** (re-explore theories/variables/datasets/methods; most often a dataset swap).

It never invents data and never fabricates a measure; a synthetic exploration surface is labeled as such, never analyzed for findings, and **never the basis for a validity verdict** (see Phase 3 and Phase 4).

## When this skill fires

In **planning regime, at Stage 5** — after the three research-question paragraphs are written (Stages 2–4 — topic, core-methods, extension-methods) and before the literature reviews (Stages 6–8). Dispatched **mentor → research agent** on a concrete handoff (cross-agent obligation **R13**): the literal `research` command plus a mentor-composed prompt naming the research questions, the constructs to pin, and the candidate data/measures surfaced during the Stage-1 exploration. It can re-fire if a later planning-regime revision (the research questions, or the method-approach) makes the operationalization stale (§7), or if `source-and-prepare-data` surfaces a measurement problem in execution.

Placed early on purpose: the validity verdict can **bounce the design back to Stage 1** — most often a dataset swap, sometimes a research-question or method reconsideration — which is cheapest to discover before the lit reviews, gaps, and structure work — "reach is a design property, checked early, not a verdict delivered late."

## The seven-step shape (how the phases run)

This skill runs the same **seven-step loop** as the execution/prep skills (`design/execution-plan-model-2026-07-07.md`),
mapped onto its five phases. It is **lighter** because it is planning-regime: its "execute" is the validity check, not a
full analysis, and its "report" is the **Data Validity Log** (`data/data-validity-log.md`), a lighter workbench log rather than a full analysis log. The gate is at the **plan**
level, not per phase.

1. **Plan.** The agent writes an **operationalization + validation plan whose purpose is to teach**: the construct
   inventory (Phase 1) and candidate measures/datasets (Phase 2), the **shape-appropriate validity check it will run**
   (Phases 3–4), the **specific code it intends to write and run** if any (e.g., a codebook parse, an
   internal-consistency reliability computation, a small exploration script), any **human-in-the-loop step** written as
   *"you have to do this for me — here are the steps"* (e.g., a credentialed codebook the student must download), and it
   **schedules its two Review-Agent checkpoints** (steps 3 and 7).
2. **Teach.** The agent teaches the plan — what each construct must capture, how the field measures it, what the validity
   check will actually decide — until the student **signals understanding**. (Scripted moment 1 lives here.)
3. **Review-Agent plan review.** The agent **composes a full handoff prompt** — pointing the **Review Agent** at the
   operationalization-and-validation plan in the Data Validity Log and the research questions in `project_design.md`, and
   instructing it to **save a dated review `.md` in `reviews/`** (e.g., `reviews/2026-05-14-operationalization-plan-review.md`) —
   which the student pastes to the Review Agent to review the *operationalization plan*. The student signals when it is
   done; the agent **retrieves the dated review file**, revises, and **re-teaches** as needed.
4. **Approve (the plan).** The reviewed plan is approved **jointly by the student (understanding) and the teacher**
   before the validity work runs — the process touchpoint, distinct from the teacher-consultation gate on the final
   operationalization *decision*.
5. **Execute (the validity check).** The agent **stages the exploration surface** (Phase 3) and **runs the
   measurement-validity check** (Phase 4) — reading the codebook, computing any reliability/exploration code directly.
   The student directs and performs any human-in-the-loop step (a gated codebook to fetch). *(The toy-set integrity
   guard binds here: a synthetic surface can shape what to try, but never certifies a measure — see Phase 3.)*
6. **Report.** Phase 5's **Data Validity Log** written to `data/data-validity-log.md` **is the report**:
   the construct→measure mapping, the exact measures, the validity verdict, the limitations, and any revision the check
   forced (a dataset switch recorded as a teachable decision).
7. **Review-Agent Quality Control (QC) audit.** The agent **composes a second full handoff prompt** — pointing the
   **Review Agent** at the Data Validity Log and the codebook excerpts in `data/codebook/`, and instructing it to **save a
   dated audit `.md` in `reviews/`** (e.g., `reviews/2026-05-14-operationalization-qc-audit.md`) — which the student
   pastes to the Review Agent for a **QC audit**, flagging anything unexpected and suggesting **3–5 spot-checks the
   student runs themselves** against the validity work. The agent **retrieves the dated audit file** and **prompts the
   student to report back what their spot-checks turned up so it can log it.** A flag that compromises the verdict loops
   back (re-check + re-report); a small one is fixed and re-reported.

*(The plan review returns to the **research agent** — it owns the plan. The whole skill still **returns the student to
the mentor** at the end on the validity verdict, per R13 and "The decision and the handoff" below.)*

## What "operationalize" means depends on the method — the menu-shape layer

The five-phase spine below is **menu-agnostic**: its *function* is constant — turn each construct into a defensible measure and validate it on a real basis before sealing. But what each phase concretely *produces*, and especially what the Phase-4 validity check *is*, depends on the method the student chose in the initiation methods-setup. Reading the 14-item methods menu, the operationalization forms fall into six shapes. (Codebook-reading is only the first; treating it as universal is the over-fit this layer exists to prevent.)

**A. Map construct → variable/index in existing data** — *menu: sophisticated regression; the data layer of social epidemiology, econometrics, geo-aware.* Operationalize: choose the variable/index, its coding/scale, and (multilevel) its level; build any derived scale. Validity: codebook fit **plus internal-consistency reliability** of constructed indices. *(Fully specified below — gold exemplar #1; Gimbrone, Rohrer.)*

**B. Operationalize the *identification*, not just the variables** — *menu: causal inference designs; social epidemiology; the identification half of econometrics.* Operationalize: the estimand **plus** the identification machinery — instrument, running variable + cutoff (RD), treatment timing (DiD), donor pool (synthetic control), the back-door adjustment set from the DAG. Validity: **assumption checks** — instrument relevance/exclusion, parallel pre-trends, RD continuity/no-manipulation, no collider in the adjustment set. This is *identification* validity — a different question from "is the variable in the data." *(Sketch; Card–Krueger, Abadie, Rohrer-2018 DAGs.)*

**C. Build a theoretical quantity by a defined construction** — *menu: econometrics; index-building in social epi and geo.* Operationalize: construct the object itself by a stated formula — a rank-rank mobility slope, a shift-share exposure, a redlining overlay. Validity: does the constructed quantity capture the theoretical object, and (for econ) does its identifying variation look exogenous; panel balance. **A/C boundary** (for indices): file by whether the *formula itself carries the theoretical claim* — a rank-rank slope or shift-share (the formula *is* the construct) is C; a **reliability-bearing latent-construct scale** (items standing in for one latent trait, validated by internal consistency) is A. A simple additive index sits on the line — e.g., an **ACE score** is a cumulative-exposure count (C, validated by content coverage), not a reflective scale (A). *(Sketch; Chetty–Hendren, Autor–Dorn–Hanson.)*

**D. A measurement model that extracts the construct from unstructured data** — *menu: NLP / computational text; modern computational / ML-as-measure; network analysis.* Operationalize: a model/dictionary/embedding/classifier (text), a prediction target + features (ML), or a **tie-definition + structural measure** (networks). Validity: **agreement with human gold labels / held-out accuracy / convergent validity** — and, for networks, the homophily-vs-influence problem. *(Sketch; Garg, Voigt, Salganik, Kosinski, Christakis–Fowler.)*

**E. Persona / synthetic generation / simulation** — *menu: synthetic AI survey participants; model fine-tuning; generative agent-based simulation.* Operationalize: a persona/conditioning + the elicited or emergent measure (silicon-sample responses, a fine-tuned-model probe, agent rules + an emergent outcome). Validity: **algorithmic fidelity / empirical calibration** — reproduce a known real benchmark for the cases you *can* check before trusting the novel outputs (the SOUL names validation as the hard part for simulation). *(Model fine-tuning straddles D and E — its probe looks D-like, but its validity step is E's: validate the fine-tuned-model probe against a real benchmark, e.g. Jiang's CommunityLM probed against ANES.)* *(Sketch; Argyle, Horton, Jiang, Park, OASIS.)*

**F. Operationalize the structure-discovery setup** — *menu: causal-graphical modeling and discovery.* Operationalize: the variable set + the discovery configuration (PC/FCI/GES/LiNGAM, the independence tests, causal-sufficiency vs. allow-hidden-confounders). Validity: **equivalence-class honesty** (report what the data can and cannot orient) + structural stability under resampling + sensitivity to the assumptions — "how much the data supports the graph," not "here is the graph." *(Sketch; Zhu–Tay–Zhang, Colineaux.)*

*(Manipulation-based primary data collection — fielded surveys and experiments — is deliberately de-emphasized in this curriculum; the quasi-experimental identification work lives in shape B on observational data. If a student and teacher choose a fielded design anyway, operationalization is instrument/manipulation design with pilot / manipulation-check validity, handled as an off-menu case.)*

**Build-as-you-go flag (load-bearing).** Only **shape A is fully specified** in this draft (Phases 3–4 are written for it, because gold exemplar #1 is observational). **Shapes B–F are sketches** — each is fleshed out to full operational detail **when a gold exemplar in that shape is built** (the curriculum's exemplar-driven design; decision-log 2026-06-27). The planned exemplars #2 (neighborhood/mobility, Chetty–Hendren lineage) and #3 (AI-adoption/employment) will specify shapes **C** (constructed quantities — mobility/exposure) and **B** (identification — natural-experiment / DiD), the two shapes nearest exemplar #1. Until a shape has an exemplar, a project in it runs the spine using the shape's sketch **plus a declared deferred-scope note** in the Data Validity Log — so the gap is explicit, never latent. *(Tracked: per-shape operationalization forms, fleshed out per exemplar — research-agent build.)*

## The five phases

### Phase 1 — Construct inventory from the research questions
List every construct the three research questions turn on, and for each write the operationalization the project *needs* — in the student's own terms, **before** any dataset or instrument constrains the answer. (In the observational case the constructs are the **exposure**, the **outcome**, and the key **covariates/confounders** — that vocabulary is shape A's instance; a text-analysis project inventories the constructs its **measurement model** must capture, a simulation project the constructs its **agents/personas** must instantiate, a discovery project the variables whose **causal structure** is in question.) This needs-first inventory is what makes the Phase-4 check checkable rather than post-hoc.

> **Scripted moment 1 — naming what must be measured** *(voice-read approved 2026-06-27).* Intent: get the student to state what each construct must capture before any dataset constrains it (ask, don't supply).
> *Agent:* "Before we look at a single dataset, say in your own words what each of your constructs would have to capture to count as measured. Start with [construct]. If someone handed you a number for it, what would that number need to reflect for you to trust it as *this* construct and not a near-cousin of it? For instance, if your construct were something general like 'civic engagement,' a number for it would have to capture what people actually *do* — voting, volunteering, turning up — not just how attached they say they feel to their community. Don't reach for a variable yet — describe the thing itself: what it includes, what it leaves out, over what unit and what stretch of time. We'll go one construct at a time. Once we've named what each has to capture, *then* we find out which datasets can actually deliver it — and we'll be able to tell honestly when one can't."

### Phase 2 — Candidate measures and candidate datasets
Draw on two sources already in hand: the **Core-Methods and Extension-Methods streams' *structure models*** (inherited with the core + extension method-approach from initiation — e.g., Gimbrone for a regression core — how the field operationalizes this exact outcome), and the **candidate data/measures** surfaced during the Stage-1 exploration. Produce a small mapping: *construct → the measure the field uses → candidate dataset(s)/instrument that could carry it → which variable/item, if known.* (Distinguish a **structure model** — how to measure/build — from a **finding model** — the published result your eventual finding is read against; §2 reference-article sub-roles.)

### Phase 3 — Secure or create an exploration surface
So the student can try operationalizations **hands-on** rather than on paper, the research agent stages one of, in order of preference (observational/shape-A case; the analogue for other shapes is the appropriate real basis — pre-trend/identification evidence for B, a gold-labeled sample for D, a calibration benchmark for E, the discovery dataset for F):

- the **codebook / data dictionary** for a candidate dataset (always pull this — it is the basis of the Phase-4 verdict);
- a **small real sample** (a public extract / a few rows) where one is openly available;
- a **clearly-labeled toy or synthetic dataset**, shaped like the real one, when no codebook or sample is reachable yet.

> **The integrity guard (load-bearing).** A toy/synthetic dataset is for **exploring operationalizations only.** It is labeled synthetic wherever it is written, is **never analyzed for findings**, is **never** a stand-in for the real data — **and is never the basis for a validity verdict.** A toy set can shape *what to try*; it cannot *certify* that a measure is sound. An operationalization explored only against synthetic data is **unvalidated** — it routes to the gated-but-real / research-teacher path (below), and the §10 state is **not** set to `validated`. This is the research-agent SOUL's "I never invent data" commitment, extended: the agent also never certifies a measure's validity against invented data. *(Distinct from `generate-synthetic-data` — the execution skill that generates synthetic/simulated data **as the legitimate studied object** when the **sealed methodology** specifies it (shape E and the cross-shape synthetic uses), validated by algorithmic fidelity. That is a different regime from this planning-time explore-only toy; the dividing line is the method — synthetic-as-object must be the chosen, sealed design, never a fallback for unreachable real data. The three-way synthetic-data distinction: decision-log 2026-06-29.)* *(Scripted moment 2 — stating the guard when a synthetic set is created; voice-read approved 2026-06-27.)* *Agent:* "There's no reachable real sample for this yet, so I've built you a small **synthetic** dataset — made-up rows shaped like the real thing — so you can try codings and see what each would look like. Two things I have to be straight about. First: this is for *trying things*, not *finding things* — no number off it is real, and none of it goes in your paper. Second: it can't tell us whether your measure is sound; only the real codebook or a real sample can do that. So if we only ever explore on this toy set, your operationalization stays **unvalidated** until we get the real data or your research teacher or faculty mentor points us to it. It helps us think; it doesn't certify anything."

### Phase 4 — The measurement-validity check (the heart of the skill)
Verify the planned operationalization **actually captures** each construct, before sealing. The *form* of the check depends on the method (the six shapes in the menu-shape layer): for a **secondary dataset** (A) it is reading the codebook (the worked case here); for a **causal design** (B) it is the identification-assumption checks (parallel trends, instrument exclusion, no collider); for a **measurement-model-on-text/data** method (D) it is agreement with human gold labels / held-out accuracy; for a **synthetic or simulation** method (E) it is algorithmic fidelity against a real benchmark; for **structure discovery** (F) it is equivalence-class honesty + stability.

**Secondary-dataset form (fully specified).** Verify the dataset measures each construct — by **reading the codebook, not the dataset's reputation.** Three questions per construct: (a) is there a variable for it at all? (b) is it measured the way the project needs — or could it be transformed to fit — units, scale, reference period, who was asked? (c) is it present for the analytic sample — the right years, the right subsample/forms? *(Transforming/recoding to fit is fine; transforming so far that it changes what the variable measures is the "operationalization would distort the construct" failure — see edge cases.)*

> **Worked example (gold exemplar #1).** The project first named **Monitoring the Future** to mirror the Gimbrone structure model. The codebook check found MTF's exposure core is *substance use*; its social-media/screen-time items sit only on particular questionnaire **forms** in particular **years** — so the obvious MTF exposure variables do **not** measure the project's construct. Verdict: poor operationalization fit as named. Resolution: **switch to YRBS**, which carries a screen-time/social-media item *and* the persistent-sadness outcome in one US high-school instrument — keeping Gimbrone as the regression *structure* model and adding Kelly (UK MCS) as the *finding* model. The check doing its job, and the reconsideration recorded as a teachable decision.

**The verdict requires a real basis.** A `validated` verdict must rest on the **real codebook** (or a real sample; or, for the non-secondary families, the real instrument pilot / manipulation check / annotation reliability). A toy/synthetic exploration alone never yields a validated verdict — it yields *unvalidated*, which routes to the gated-but-real / research-teacher path (Phase 3 guard; edge cases).

This is a genuine high-judgment moment — a construct measured badly can sink an otherwise good design — so at the verdict the skill surfaces the research agent's **careful-tier** option (the SOUL's "How I run, and when to use a stronger model": suggest switching to the careful tier for this call, then dropping back). *(It does **not** reference the mentor's `model-escalation` meta-skill — that ships only in the project-mentor profile and names mentor-specific model tiers; the research agent's escalation surface is its own SOUL, which keeps the tiers model-agnostic. The escalation-policy question is a Tier-2 flag, above.)* Fire it once, at the verdict — not per phase.

> **Scripted moment 3 — the validity verdict** *(voice-read approved 2026-06-27).* Intent: the teaching beat — walk the student through the measurement-validity check, name the verdict (advice, not a verdict), and surface the careful tier when the call is load-bearing. Distinct from the escalation hook itself.
> *Agent:* "Here's the call that decides whether this design stands: does this dataset actually *measure* what your question is about? We read the codebook, not the dataset's reputation. For each construct, three questions — is there a variable for it at all; is it measured the way your question needs, or could it be transformed to fit (units, scale, time window, who was asked); and is it there for the years and the sample you'd actually analyze? Read your exposure's entry with me, line by line. … [where it falls short] Here's what I'm seeing: [the gap]. My read is that this variable doesn't measure your construct — but that's my read, not a verdict; if your research teacher sees it differently, we go with that. This is exactly the kind of call worth slowing down for — a construct measured badly can sink an otherwise good design — so before we commit, it's worth switching to the careful tier for this one decision, then dropping back. What do you see when you read the entry yourself?"

### Phase 5 — Select the dataset/measurement plan and write the operationalization spec
Choose the dataset (or fix the instrument/measurement plan) and write the **Data Validity Log** into `data/data-validity-log.md`: the construct→measure mapping, the exact measures (item, units, coding, reference window), the validity verdict, and the **limitations** the operationalization carries (e.g., YRBS has no parental-education/SES covariate — a noted limit that, if anything, sharpens the case for the extension). Set the §10 `operationalization/data-validity state` to **validated** **only if** the verdict rests on a real basis (Phase 4); otherwise leave it unvalidated and route per the bounce/gated edge cases. The covariate set and the model/estimator are *not* fixed here — they are built on these operationalized variables at Stage 14 (`design-detailed-methodology`).

## The decision and the handoff

Operationalization + dataset selection is a **major decision**: it goes into `decisions.md` with the five-field rationale (what was decided · why · alternatives rejected · uncertainty remaining · teacher-consultation) and through the **teacher-consultation gate** — the agent's fitness judgment is **advice, never a verdict**; a teacher steer is adopted, not re-litigated. On completion the skill **returns the student to the mentor** with the literal `mentor` command (R13 return direction), carrying the validated operationalization forward to the lit reviews (6–8) and, later, the methodology (14).

**Bounce-back.** If, after honest work, **no reachable dataset/measure validly captures a construct**, the return carries a **`blocked-on-upstream-reopening`** verdict targeting **Stage 1** (the reconsideration hub; dispatch contract §5.1). The common, cheap fix is a **dataset swap** made at Stage 1; sometimes it is a **research-question revision** reached *through* the re-exploration (Stage 1 → the RQ stages re-run); and, near-catastrophically, a **method change** that re-enters the **initiation methods-setup**. The §7 stale map cascades the downstream rewrites either way.

## Edge cases (initial)
- **No dataset/measure validly captures the construct.** Bounce-back to the mentor — a **Stage-1 bounce** (re-explore datasets/variables/methods); not a dead stop — an in-reach reframing of the construct or an alternative dataset/measure is sought first.
- **The construct has no pre-existing variable — the measure must be built.** Common outside shape A: an annotation scheme or classifier (D), a constructed quantity by formula (C), a persona/probe (E), or a discovery setup (F) — Stage-1 exploration (its sub-step 1b) explicitly anticipates "constructs that would need inventing." Operationalizing here *is* building the measure, and the validity check is the shape-appropriate one (gold-label reliability; does-the-formula-capture-the-object; algorithmic fidelity; equivalence-class honesty) — per the menu-shape layer. Until that shape has a gold exemplar, run its sketch with a declared deferred-scope note.
- **Two defensible operationalizations.** Surface the trade-off (what each measures, what each misses) and record the choice with its rationale; the road not taken is a limitation for the Discussion.
- **A needed covariate is absent from the dataset.** Record it as a named limitation that feeds the Stage-14 methodology and the Discussion — not a reason to abandon the dataset if exposure + outcome are validly measured.
- **Dataset is reachable but gated beyond the student's access.** Record the block specifically; route to the research teacher or faculty mentor (institutional access may exist); never substitute an unofficial copy.
- **Operationalization would distort the construct.** If the only way to make a variable usable changes what it measures, that is a validity failure surfacing late — return within Phases 4–5 and surface it; a late validity failure is a finding to report, not something to work around.
- **A problem surfaces in execution.** `source-and-prepare-data` re-invokes this skill; but note the seal rules (§6) — post-seal, the **methods and operationalization evolve** as the real data shows what they support, and the student **keeps the relevant paper paragraph current with what they actually did**; the one thing frozen is the **Hypotheses and/or Expected Findings** (you never change your predictions after seeing the data). Keeping that paragraph current with the real work is exactly what a strong study does.
- **Student data / PII.** Out of scope by rule — this skill operationalizes against public research datasets; student-generated data never enters shared analysis or any model.
