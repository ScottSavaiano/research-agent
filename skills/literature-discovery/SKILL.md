## What this skill is

The **first** of the literature-review trio. It **assembles the candidate article set** for the student's stage-7 literature review — it does not decide inclusion (that is `literature-filter-and-verify`) and does not read the articles (that is `paper-engagement`). Its job is breadth and reach: surface every article that could belong on the student's reading lists, from every channel available, so the filter step has a real field to work from.

The literature review has **two aspects**, and so do its reading lists (the educator's two-aspect formalization, 2026-06-29; mirrors the two syntheses `conduct-literature-review` already owns):

- **The topic reading list** — studies of the student's *subject* (the Topic-related Research Question). Read later for what they found.
- **The methods reading list** — studies examined for *how* they did it (the Methods-related Research Question). **Built first from the topic corpus itself** — the topic studies are the first place mined for methods, so an article that serves both aspects is dual-purposed, not re-found — and **supplemented** with method-exemplar / curriculum-corpus articles only where the topic corpus does not carry the student's method (e.g., the method is new to this literature). This keeps the methods corpus anchored on the topic corpus, which is what eliminates the empty-methods-corpus failure mode (`conduct-literature-review`; educator ruling 2026-06-04).

The corpus **may legitimately outgrow its seed** — the reference articles are the compass, not the territory (R5; the SOUL's "your compass into the literature").

## When this skill fires

In **planning regime, stage 7**, on the mentor's `conduct-literature-review` handoff (R1): the student pastes the mentor-composed prompt that points at the reading briefs (`project_design.md → ## Cycle N — Reading Briefs`), the research questions, and `reference_articles.md`. The skill reads those named files first (R1/R9), then assembles candidates. It can re-fire if the briefs are revised, or when the student returns with more supplied articles mid-reading.

## The four discovery channels

Discovery draws on four channels, run with **topic-first** sequencing (assemble the topic list, then build the methods list off it — see above).

**1. The curriculum-owned REST search.** The skill's `scripts/` REST client queries **OpenAlex, Crossref, and Semantic Scholar** directly (no third-party MCP; decision-log 2026-06-27). It is **keyless / polite-pool** with a **program `mailto` identity** (OpenAlex + Crossref polite pool; Semantic Scholar keyless at lower limits, optional key for higher). Queries are seeded from the **topic brief** and the **reference-article bibliographies** (forward/backward citation chasing via OpenAlex/S2 references + citations). Concurrency is a non-issue — students work asynchronously across the semester (educator, 2026-06-27). The script exposes a **discover** operation and a **resolve/verify** operation; `literature-filter-and-verify` reuses the latter so verification is not reimplemented.

**2. The curriculum corpus (prioritized — especially the methods list).** The curated trove — the `curriculum-articles` repo, manifested by `method-exemplars.yaml` and `structure-models.yaml`, keyed by method family — is examined and its matches **prioritized**, because the student's method-approach (chosen with the mentor at the methods menu) maps directly onto method-family exemplars. These are the **method exemplars** `conduct-literature-review` already names as the methods-synthesis seed, and they connect to `operationalize-construct`'s structure models. Reached **two ways** (educator, 2026-06-29): (a) corpus articles the **mentor names in the handoff prompt** (the mentor knows the method-menu choice), and (b) the skill's **own manifest scan** matching the method-approach — belt-and-suspenders, so a corpus match the mentor didn't name is not missed.

**3. Student/mentor-supplied articles.** The student, the research teacher, or another mentor will often already have articles — on either aspect. The skill **actively prompts** for them (scripted moment below) and **ingests** them two ways: a **PDF dropped into the project folder**, or an **`add this <DOI/URL/citation>`** command (which runs `fetch-articles`, R7a, plus the metadata/verify step). Supplied articles enter the same `literature-filter-and-verify` pipeline and land on the right reading list, **flagged as student/mentor-supplied** — a neutral provenance tag (where an article came from), **not** surfaced to students as a contribution-value distinction: selecting and synthesizing the whole corpus is the student's work regardless of who first surfaced a given paper.

**4. The reference-article starter set.** The Project Reference Articles already in `reference_articles.md` are the seed compass — included by default, their bibliographies feeding channel 1.

## What this skill writes to the workspace
- **The two reading lists** (topic + methods) — candidate entries with their metadata (title, authors, year, venue, DOI, abstract, source-API/corpus/supplied **provenance**, citation count) and the **dual-purpose cross-flag** where an article serves both — discovery *owns* this flag (it builds the methods list off the topic list, so it is the skill that knows an article serves both); `literature-filter-and-verify` surfaces it as the 🔁 logo and may *additionally* set it for an article that ranks onto both lists at filter time (F1.3). *(Home — extend `reference_articles.md` vs. a dedicated reading-list surface — is the §11 flag above; proposed: a reading-list surface.)* It writes **candidates**, not the final included set — `literature-filter-and-verify` ranks, verifies, and triages them; `paper-engagement` reads them.
- **`articles/`** — any supplied PDFs the student adds are staged here (the `fetch-articles` staging convention); raw PDFs git-ignored as elsewhere.

## Cross-agent handoff
Inbound from the mentor at stage 7 (R1). Hands the assembled candidate set **forward to `literature-filter-and-verify`** (the next trio skill). Uses **`fetch-articles` (R7a, re-bundled into the research profile)** to stage supplied PDFs and resolve "add this" references. It does **not** return the student to the mentor — that return happens at the end of the trio, after `paper-engagement` (R1 return for the synthesis conversation).

## Edge cases (initial)
- **Thin reference seed.** If the bibliographies don't open into a real reading list, discovery works from the **brief itself** rather than the seeds (the `conduct-literature-review` thin-seed edge case) — the corpus outgrows its seed (R5).
- **A supplied PDF with no clean metadata.** Extract what is present and resolve it against the APIs (the script's resolve operation) so it carries a DOI/record for the verify step. If it **resolves**, it is `real ✓` like any other candidate; if it **cannot be resolved**, it still enters (the student holds the file) but in the distinct **`supplied — unverified`** verification state — **never** `real ✓` — carried to `literature-filter-and-verify`, which surfaces it as unverified with a nudge to confirm the source with the teacher. *(F1.1, 2026-06-29: the file existing is not the work being a confirmed real publication.)*
- **A corpus/supplied article duplicates an API hit.** Dedupe by DOI; keep the richest-provenance record (supplied/corpus provenance is retained over a bare API hit).
- **The method is absent from the topic corpus.** Not a gap in discovery — it's the signal to pull method-exemplar/curriculum-corpus articles onto the methods list (channel 2), exactly the case those exist for.
- **Student data / PII / paywall.** Discovery surfaces public scholarship only; "add this" obeys `fetch-articles`' 403-is-final / no-paywall-circumvention rule (the access question is `literature-filter-and-verify`'s triage).
